Sensors & Actuators

A full reference guide for common sensors and output modules

🔍 Distance & Proximity Sensors

HC-SR04
Ultrasonic distance sensor. Measures 2cm–400cm using sound waves. Easy to use with Arduino.
Beginner 5V
VL53L0X
Laser-based ToF sensor. Very accurate, communicates via I2C. Great for robotics.
Intermediate 3.3V

🌡️ Temperature & Humidity Sensors

DHT22
Reads temperature and humidity digitally. More accurate than DHT11. Ideal for weather stations.
Beginner 3.3–5V
DS18B20
Waterproof temperature sensor using OneWire protocol. Used in water temperature monitoring.
Intermediate 3.3–5V

📡 Motion & Accelerometer Sensors

PIR HC-SR501
Passive infrared sensor. Detects motion from warm bodies. Used in alarm systems.
Beginner 5V
ADXL345
3-axis accelerometer. Detects tilt and vibration. Communicates via I2C or SPI.
Intermediate 3.3V

Wiring Tips

  • Always check your sensor's operating voltage before connecting (3.3V vs 5V)
  • Use a level shifter when connecting 5V sensors to a 3.3V board like ESP32
  • Capacitors near power pins help reduce signal noise in analog sensors
  • Pull-up resistors are often needed for I2C communication lines
  • Use short, shielded wires for sensitive analog sensors to reduce interference

⚠️ Important: Connecting a 5V sensor directly to an ESP32 GPIO pin can permanently damage the board. Always verify voltage compatibility first.
